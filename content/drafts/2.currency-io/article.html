The major conceptual difference between a webapp and an app has always been primarily defined by the browser chrome (toolbars, navigation buttons, address bar, etc.) wrapped around it. A few years ago, the page refresh was a defining factor as well, but the onset of ajax has helped negate this.

For mobile, the combination of two relatively recent inventions, iOS’ ‘Add to Home Screen’ functionality and implementation of html5’s offline caching, has opened up the potential to build quite full-featured webapps that can, in theory, do as much as a cocoa codebase.

This opens up some interesting opportunities as the power and speed that a single codebase gives you cannot be underestimated. If rather than maintaining separate iPhone, iPad, Android and web-based applications, a company like twitter was able to dedicate itself to just one, then its resources could be working much more directly on the improvement of the whole service. Rather than constantly playing feature catchup amongst all of its splintered codebases.

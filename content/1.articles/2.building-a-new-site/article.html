Over the past few years our work together as ab+c has progressively grown from a side project to the point where most of the work we do is under the ab+c banner. It became clear that our one-page website was no longer going to cut it, so we started throwing around some ideas.

The starting point was an article we co-wrote about our W3C restyle project on Ben’s blog. Having the two authors, we were interested in making both voices obvious to the reader.

There was something about that style of writing that just felt right. In our work together we don’t buy into the idea of a unified voice, we each bring our own ideas and perspectives and feel strongly enough to argue about them. Our intention for this site is to expose some of these exchanges. This is where our most interesting ideas come from.

Design & Build

As far as the design, I’ve kept it straightforward. Its purpose is to present our text honestly without getting in the reader’s way. We are both represented throughout the site by unique typefaces, which were chosen to reflect the typographic styles of our personal sites. I’ve been messing around with METAFONT recently, so it was also a nice opportunity to make use of a variant of Donald Knuth’s Computer Modern.

Our philosophy is that different grades of browsers should experience our content differently, so the css has been structured accordingly. The grid and typography is optimised for iOS devices through some custom @media queries and we’re using an alternate, simplified stylesheet for Internet Explorer (or using Chrome Frame if it’s available).

Behind the scenes we’re running the site on stacey with deployment through a shared, linked dropbox folder. It automatically copies my changes straight across to Ben’s local copy and the server at the same time. We have traditionally used git and Heroku to manage all of our deployment, so this file-based system makes for an interesting alternative.
